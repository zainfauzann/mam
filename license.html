Step-by-step

How to apply

10 – 14 working days
01 / Eligibility

Check eligibility

Driver and rider licences are issued from age 14 (karting) and 16 (full national). Officials must have completed an Officials' Clinic in the prior 24 months. Entrants must be registered with SSM as a Malaysian business entity.

Self-check
02 / Medical

Complete the medical

All four-wheel applicants complete the FIA Annex L medical examination at an MAM-approved panel clinic. Two-wheel applicants complete the FIM equivalent. The medical certificate is valid for 12 months and must be current at the time of submission.

Panel clinic list in Documents
03 / Documents

Gather required documents

National ID (MyKad / passport), two passport-size photographs, completed application form (DCL-26 / RCL-26 / OFC-26 / ENT-26), valid medical certificate, and proof of inscription payment. Renewals additionally submit the previous licence card.

Form pack in Documents
04 / Submit

Submit online or in person

Submit through the online intake below, by email to licences@mam.com.my, or in person at the MAM Secretariat in Kuala Lumpur. Online submissions are acknowledged within 24 hours.

Online preferred
05 / Review

Secretariat review

The MAM Secretariat verifies documentation and medical clearance. Officials licences additionally route through the Officials Commission for grade assignment. Most reviews close within 10 working days; complex cases (international upgrade, appeal) may take longer.

≈ 10 – 14 days
06 / Issuance

Licence issued

Approved licences are issued as both a physical card and a digital licence record. Holders receive an email with the digital record and a courier-delivered physical card within 5 working days of approval. The licence is valid until 31 December of the cycle year.

FAQ

Common questions

How long does it take to receive my licence?

Most applications close within 10 to 14 working days. Officials licences and international grade upgrades can take longer because they involve a secondary review by the Officials Commission or FIA / FIM. Submitting online and including a current medical typically gets you to the fastest end of the range.

Can I use my licence outside Malaysia?

National licences (Grade C / B / A) are valid only inside Malaysia. International FIA / FIM grades — issued on top of a current national licence — are required for ARRC, MotoGP qualifying events, FIA-recognised rally rounds, and most overseas series. Upgrade pathway runs through MAM, then FIA or FIM Asia.

What if I'm under 18?

Minors aged 14 to 17 may apply with parent or guardian consent. Karting licences are available from 14 with the appropriate FIA Karting medical. Two-wheel and full four-wheel competition licences are issued from 16 onwards, with parental indemnity required until the holder reaches 18.

Do I need a road driver's licence too?

A road driver's licence (Malaysian Class D or equivalent) is required for most four-wheel categories at Grade B and above, and for all open-cockpit and high-performance categories. Karting and closed-course racing categories do not require a road licence.

How do I renew before the deadline?

Renewal opens 1 December and closes 31 January with a -20% early-bird discount. Late renewals after 28 February incur a reinstatement fee. Submit through this online intake under "Renewal" with your previous licence number — you don't need to repeat the personal-details section if your record is current.

Need help applying?

The MAM Secretariat can walk you through eligibility, medical clearance and the application portal. Most enquiries are answered within one working day.
