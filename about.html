Our story

One association,
every discipline.

MAM is the official sanctioning body for motorsports in Malaysia, recognised by both the FIA and FIM. As the governing body of Malaysian motorsports, it is MAM's duty and obligation to ensure motorsports develop in Malaysia and continue to excel at all levels.

"To ensure all motorsports events in Malaysia are sanctioned for the benefit and safety of every driver and rider."

Incorporated on 28 February 2007 as Persatuan Sukan Permotoran Malaysia, MAM works with the FIA, FIM Asia, the Ministry of Youth and Sports (KBS) and a network of 80+ affiliate clubs across all 13 states — covering every discipline from grassroots karting to continental championships at Sepang.

Heritage

Seven decades, one mission

1963

The National Expansion

Following the formation of the Federation of Malaysia, the primary automotive body (originally the Automobile Association of Malaya) is rechartered to cover the newly joined states of Sabah and Sarawak — establishing the first unified national framework for road rules and competitive racing across the country.

1985

International recognition & Shah Alam

The opening of the Shah Alam Circuit (Batu Tiga) marks Malaysia's entry into international-grade racing. The governing bodies of this era oversee the transition from local club racing to hosting the World Sportscar Championship and later the Malaysian Motorcycle Grand Prix.

1999

The Sepang revolution

The inauguration of Sepang International Circuit (SIC) fundamentally changes the landscape. With the arrival of Formula 1, the need for a highly specialised sporting authority becomes paramount — and technical and sporting management aligns strictly with FIA and FIM standards.

2007

Formal incorporation of MAM

The Motorsports Association of Malaysia is officially incorporated on 28 February 2007 — a modern, streamlined governing body dedicated specifically to the sporting side of motorsports, separate from general automobile associations.

2019

Transition of authority

The FIA and FIM officially recognise MAM as Malaysia's sole motorsports governing body, transferring sanctioning authority from the Automobile Association of Malaysia (AAM) to MAM — solidifying MAM as the only body authorised to sanction races and issue competition licences in Malaysia.

2026

Today's roadmap

MAM operates as a robust ecosystem focused on long-term sustainability — the National Karting framework feeding talent into global ladders, 1,200+ licence holders and 80+ affiliated clubs under one code, and the renewed 2026–2030 FIM cycle anchoring Malaysia as an Asia-Pacific racing hub.
